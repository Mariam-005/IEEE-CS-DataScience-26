## Descriptive Statistics :
used to summarize, organize and describe the main features of a dataset
-Quickly understand a dataset
but!
-Outliers can skew the mean

## Central Tendency:
- Mean = average
    
- Median =  value in the middle in sorted values
    
- Mode = most frequent val

EX: 5, 7, 7, 9, 12 
	mean=(5+7+7+9+12)/5 =8
	median=7
	mode=7

## Measures of Spread:
- Range = max value − min value
    =12-5=7
- Variance = avg squared distance from mean
    =5.6
- Std Dev = avg distance from mean
    =2.37
## Shape of Data
- Skewness  of data tilt (left/right)



## Variance:
measures how far the data values are from the mean
## **Useful**
- To compare how spread out two datasets are
**but**
  not ideal if data is highly skewed


# Inferential Statistics :
uses data from a sample to make predictions about a larger population
## **can be misleading** :
If the sample is biased
If outliers affect the results


## Quartiles :
divide ordered data into 4 equal parts 
helps understand how the data is distributed

- Detecting outliers
    
- Understanding distribution shape
    
- Used in boxplots


types :
* **Q1 (First Quartile)**= 25% of data below it
    
- **Q2 (Median)** =50% of data below it
    
- **Q3 (Third Quartile)** = 75% of data below it*


EX: 10, 20, 30, 40, 50
q2= median =30
q1=in the lower half = 15
q3=in the upper half= 45

## Interquartile Range (IQR)
the spread of the middle 50% of the data
IQR=Q3-Q1=45-15=30
